### Important
{: .no_toc }

<p align="center"><small>Shows are organised alphabetically (ignoring articles) and all videos are in chronological order under each title, unless I have split them further by tour.</small></p>

Do keep in mind there's still a chance I have missed or mislabelled something, in which case I will offer a replacement and update you when I find the correct copy.</small></p>

<p align="center"><small>If you have a better copy than my existing one (missing tracks, larger file size, original untracked files etc) please do contact me about it. I would love to trade for them!</small></p>

#### 9 to 5: The Musical - Pre-Broadway / Los Angeles - September 20, 2008 (Opening Night)
{: .d-inline-block }

juniper47
{: .label .label-blue }

FORMAT
: MP3 `45 tracks`

CAST
: Allison Janney (Violet Newstead), Megan Hilty (Doralee Rhodes), Stephanie J Block (Judy Bernly), Kathy Fitzgerald (Roz Keith), Marc Kudisch (Franklin Hart, Jr.), Andy Karl (Joe), Charlie Pollock (Dwayne), Dan Cooney (Dick), Van Hughes (Josh), Ioana Alfonso (Maria), Jeremy Davis (Bob Enright), Ann Harada (Kathy), Lisa Howard (Missy), Michael X Martin (Tinsworthy), Karen Murphy (Margaret), Tory Ross (Daphne), Maia Nkenge Wilson (Anita), Autumn Guzzardi, Brandi Wooten, Brendan King, Jessica Lea Patty, Justin Bohon, Kevin Kern, Mark Myers, Michael Mindlin, Paul Castree, Wayne Schroder

NOTES
: Opening night insanity (i.e. very enthusiastic audience involving a lot of yelling and cheering). The show stopped after technical difficulties through \"Here For You\", and again, Dolly Parton came out to talk to the audience AND sing \"9 to 5\". There are a few little bloopers and ad-libs. My favorite is when Marc accidentally knocked over the pencils when falling back in his chair, and Megan picked them up. Then when he was supposed to deliberately knock over the pencils, Megan went to pick them up again and said, \"Just keeps happening, don\'t it?\" SJB still sounds a bit sick, but she used it to her advantage and did a crazy fierce \"Get Out and Stay Out\". I was just about on the floor in amazement. A lot of little changes, too. The big one for master was that they got rid of the dialogue between Andy Karl and Allison Janney in the lunch room scene \-- now all he does is trip, and then he slinks off.
